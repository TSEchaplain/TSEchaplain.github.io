What?

The TSE chaplaincy offers a safe, confidential space to simply talk and connect for clergy who often experience a level of disconnection and isolation. This service supports those in Toronto Southeast Presbytery who are engaged in the wide and various forms of leadership in Local Ministry Units and presbyters.

I am your TSE chaplain, Michael Cottrell. I provide a place and presence of welcome, emphasizing those things that develop and nurture our life and work wherein the Spirit at the heart of life can be explored, celebrated and shared.

With an open door, attentive ear, and compassionate heart and wisdom of experience, I strive to make a difference—one conversation, one event, one gathering at a time.

More

Why?

To assist clergy and presbyters to make sense of the environment in which you currently find yourselves and to help to provide the spiritual tools/direction to enable and empower you to get the most out of your experience.

Where?

Located at the corner of Danforth & Coxwell, this space is set apart from offices and conference rooms. This is a place to talk, to relax and to remember what ignited your excitement into ministry and leadership. The space is also a safe and calm place to share grief, frustration and confusion.

Who?

I am Michael Cottrell. I engage in a Christian ministry of deep compassion and radical inclusion. Since the age of 19, I have been directly and personally engaged in expressions of the Christian faith. I bring forward my experience as a Roman Catholic monk, ordained priest in the Anglican Church of Canada and minister of sacrament and word in the United Church of Canada.

During my 2015 sabbatical, I studied the history and tradition of Mahāyāna Buddhism, focusing on its contemplative practices. Although my studies were in the branch of Tibetan Buddhism, I practice and am a member of the Zen Buddhist Temple in Toronto.

With the discipline of Buddhism and deep roots in the sacred practices of Christianity, I am grounded:

in the holy which he names Spirit,
in the teaching of Jesus Christ and
in the wisdom of the people.

I am always engaging in how both East and West spiritual practices relate to the here and now of life.

I support people to develop a deeper rhythm and practice in their daily life. I co-lead and teach mindfulness practice in a contemplative experience.

Ordained for 30 years, I have been teacher, counselor, spiritual leader, healer and therapist. I have completed post-graduate studies in psychotherapy along with a Masters of Divinity (London), undergraduate degree in Social Work (Waterloo) and a teaching certificate in Early Childhood Education and Child Development (Conestoga).

I am a registered psychotherapist. I own the clinic Be Here Now Psychotherapy. With 10 therapists, we are supporting and creating change in the lives of individuals and the community.
